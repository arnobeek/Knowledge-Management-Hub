About UN Uganda

The United Nations system in Uganda brings together resident and non-resident agencies working with government, civil society and development partners to advance the country's national priorities and the Sustainable Development Goals.

Our mandate

UN Uganda coordinates the collective work of UN agencies, funds and programmes operating in the country under a single strategic framework — the UN Sustainable Cooperation Framework (UNSDCF). This knowledge repository exists to make that collective work visible, searchable and easy to draw on for reporting, communication and evidence-informed decision-making.

Cooperation Framework overview

The UNSDCF Uganda is organised around three outcome areas. Items in the repository are tagged against these outcomes so progress and evidence can be traced back to the framework.

Outcome 1

Sustainable and inclusive growth

Supporting inclusive economic growth, decent work and resilient livelihoods, including youth employment, digital innovation and private sector partnerships.

Outcome 2

Human capital development

Strengthening health, nutrition, education and social protection systems so people can build and use their capabilities.

Outcome 3

Resilience and sustainability

Building resilience to climate change, disasters and shocks, and supporting sustainable management of natural resources.
